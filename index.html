Focus

Small systems, solved properly

I like problems that are annoying in a very specific way — a pitch double-booked at 8pm on a Friday, a phone at 40% battery with no charger in sight. Most of what I build tries to fix a piece of that properly: a booking system that never lets two people claim the same slot, a peer-to-peer network for something every student on campus eventually runs out of.

I'm currently building small, working software while filling in the foundation underneath it — algorithms, data structures, and the machine learning concepts that make the rest of it hold together. I'd rather ship something narrow and correct than something broad and hand-wavy.

TurfHub

formerly BookMyTurf

Delivered

A multi-vendor booking platform for turf football, built around a problem that's a real bug and not an edge case: two people trying to claim the same 8pm slot. Redis handles the slot-hold concurrency underneath; the frontend leans into a night-pitch, under-the-lights look.

FastAPI Redis Slot concurrency

ChargeDIU

Campus Charger Sharing Network

In transit

A peer-to-peer marketplace for something every student runs out of on campus: a charger cable, right when the battery hits 40%. Students list spare chargers — USB-C, Lightning, Micro-USB, laptop — at a flat borrow price, with zero commission on what renters earn. An ID check and a before-photo on every borrow keep a shared charger from quietly disappearing.
